- Jing determination: It is both. The flipped tile is the Primary Spirit (Zheng Jing) and the next tile in sequence is the Secondary Spirit (Fu Jing)

- Wildcard pair: Allowed. A Jing can act as a wildcard for any tile, including to form a pair. The "Spirit Fishing" (Jing Diao) rule specifically describes waiting for any tile to pair with a Jing

- Chow direction: Standard. You can only Chow from the player immediately to your left (the previous player)

- Payment cap: Uncapped. The sources describe a multiplicative system without mentioning a 64-unit limit. High-scoring hands use "Explosive Spirit" bonuses and multiple x2 multipliers

- Dealer retention: The dealer retains the seat on a Dealer Win or a Draw (Flowing Bureau). If a non-dealer wins, the dealer position rotates

- Thirteen Orphans: In Nanchang Mahjong, the traditional "Thirteen Orphans" is replaced by the Thirteen Misfits (Shi San Luan), where numbered tiles must have gaps of at least 2 (e.g., 1, 4, 7) and honors must be unique

- Scoring base: The base unit is 1 point
