# Nanchang Mahjong (南昌麻将) — Official Web App Rules

## 1\. Overview

Nanchang Mahjong is a four-player variation characterized by its "Spirit Tiles" (Jing), unique sequence rules for honors, and high-volatility scoring via multipliers.

## 2\. Equipment & Setup

### 2.1 Tile Set (136 tiles)

- **Characters (万/Wàn)**: 1–9 (4 of each)
- **Dots (筒/Tǒng)**: 1–9 (4 of each)
- **Bamboo (条/Tiáo)**: 1–9 (4 of each)
- **Winds (风/Fēng)**: East, South, West, North (4 of each)
- **Dragons (箭/Jiàn)**: Red (Zhong), Green (Fa), White (Bai) (4 of each)
- _Note: No flower or season tiles are used._ 5, 13

### 2.2 Seating & Dealing

1. **Dealer (庄家/Zhuang)**: Determined by dice rolls. The dealer starts with 14 tiles; others start with 13 5, 8\.
2. **The Wall**: 17 stacks (34 tiles) per side, forming a square 10, 14\.
3. **Rotation**: If the dealer wins or the game is a draw, the dealer stays. Otherwise, the role moves counter-clockwise 8-10.

## 3\. The Spirit Tiles (精牌 / Jīng Pái)

Spirit Tiles are the defining "wildcards" of Nanchang Mahjong.

### 3.1 Determination

After dealing, the dealer rolls two dice. The sum determines which stack (counting from the tail of the wall) is used to reveal the **Indicator Tile** 1, 2\.

- **Primary Spirit (正精/Zhèng Jīng)**: The Indicator Tile itself.
- **Secondary Spirit (副精/Fù Jīng)**: The tile one rank above the Indicator in the same suit/group.
- **Wraps**: 9 → 1 1\.
- **Winds**: East → South → West → North → East 1, 2\.
- **Dragons**: Red (Zhong) → Green (Fa) → White (Bai) → Red (Zhong) 1, 2\.

### 3.2 Wildcard Rules

- **Universal Substitute**: A Jing can replace any tile in a Chow, Pung, or Pair (except when used in a Kong) 2\.
- **Discarding**: A Jing can be discarded. Once on the table, it loses its wildcard status and is treated as its face value 3, 15\.
- **Natural Use**: If a player holds a Jing but uses it as its original value (or doesn't use it to complete the hand), the hand may qualify for the "German" bonus 3, 11\.

## 4\. Gameplay & Melds

### 4.1 Movement

Play proceeds counter-clockwise. A turn consists of Drawing, (optional) Melding, and Discarding 16, 17\.

### 4.2 Claiming Discards (Priority: Win \> Kong/Pung \> Chow)

- **Chow (吃/Chī)**: Form a sequence. Only allowed from the player to the left 5, 6\.
- **Pung (碰/Pèng)**: Form a triplet from any player's discard 5, 18\.
- **Kong (杠/Gàng)**: Form a quadruplet.
- **Exposed Kong**: From a discard or adding to an existing Pung.
- **Concealed Kong**: Drawing all four tiles yourself.
- _Note: Every Kong triggers an immediate draw from the tail of the wall_ 2, 19\.

### 4.3 Unique "Honors Straight" Rule

Unlike other variants, three **non-repeating** Wind tiles or the three different Dragon tiles can form a valid sequence (Chow) 5, 20, 21\.

## 5\. Winning Hand Types

### 5.1 Standard Hand

- **4 Melds \+ 1 Pair**: Melds can be Chows, Pungs, or Kongs 2\.

### 5.2 Special Hands

- **Small Seven Pairs (小七对/Xiǎo Qī Duì)**: Seven pairs. Must be concealed (no Chows/Pungs) 4, 11\.
- **Big Seven Pairs (大七对/Dà Qī Duì)**: Also known as **Pung Pung Hu** (All Triplets). Consists of 4 triplets/kongs and 1 pair 4\.
- **Thirteen Misfits (十三烂/Shí Sān Luàn)**: 14 tiles where numbered tiles of the same suit have an interval \> 2 (e.g., 1, 4, 7\) and all honors are unique. Must be concealed 10-12.
- **Seven Star Thirteen Misfits (七星十三烂)**: A Thirteen Misfits hand containing all 7 unique honors (E, S, W, N, Red, Green, White) 11, 12\.

## 6\. Scoring & Payouts

Nanchang scoring is divided into **Instant Payouts** and **Winning Payouts**.

### 6.1 Instant Payouts (Irrespective of Win)

These are paid immediately by all players when the event occurs:

- **Exposed/Supplement Kong**: 1 point from everyone 4, 22\.
- **Concealed Kong**: 2 points from everyone 4, 22\.
- **Sacking the Dealer (抄庄/Chāo Zhuāng)**: If all 4 players discard the same tile in the first round, the dealer pays 5 points to everyone 2, 6\.

### 6.2 Spirit Tile Payouts (End of Hand)

Every player pays others based on the Jing tiles held in their hand/melds:

- **Primary Spirit**: 2 points each 4, 7\.
- **Secondary Spirit**: 1 point each 4, 7\.
- **Spirit Kong (杠精)**: Holding all four of a single Spirit tile (as a Kong) earns 10 bonus points from everyone 4, 7\.
- **Explosive Spirit (冲关/Chòng Guān)**: If a player's total Spirit points are ≥ 5, they receive a multiplier:
- 5 pts → 10 (x2)
- 6 pts → 18 (x3)
- 7 pts → 28 (x4)
- _Formula: Points × (Points \- 3\)_ 3, 7, 23\.
- **Indomitable Spirit (霸王精/Bà Wáng Jīng)**: If only one player has Spirit tiles, their total Spirit score is doubled 3, 7\.

### 6.3 Winning Payout Table

The winner's points are calculated as: **Base (1) × Multipliers**.

- **Discard Win (胡/Hu)**: Discarder pays x2; others pay x1 4\.
- **Self-Draw (自摸/Zì Mō)**: Everyone pays x2 4\.
- **Dealer (庄家/Zhuang)**: Any points the dealer wins or loses are doubled (x2) 4\.
- **Small Seven Pairs / Thirteen Misfits / Big Seven Pairs**: x2 4, 11\.
- **Seven Star Thirteen Misfits**: x4 4, 11\.
- **Heavenly/Earthly Win (天胡/地胡)**: Flat 20 points from everyone (no other multipliers apply) 4, 11\.
- **Robbing a Kong (抢杠)**: Treated as a Self-Draw; the player attempting the Kong pays for everyone 4, 11\.

### 6.4 Win Modifiers (Stackable)

- **German (德国/Déguó)**: Win without using any Jing as wildcards.
- _Payout_: (Base x2) \+ 5 points 4, 11\.
- **True German (德中德)**: A "German" win where no other player at the table has a Jing in their hand.
- _Payout_: (Base x4) \+ 5 points 4, 11\.
- **Spirit Fishing (精钓/Jīng Diào)**: Waiting for a pair while holding 4 melds. Since the Jing is wild, any drawn tile completes the hand. Must be Self-Draw.
- _Multiplier_: x2 3, 4\.
