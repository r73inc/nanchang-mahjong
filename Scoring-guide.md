# How the Points Work — Player's Guide

A plain-language guide to scoring in Nanchang Mahjong, written for someone sitting at a real table with physical tiles. This describes the rules exactly as the app plays them.

---

## The goal

Everyone starts with the same number of points. Points only ever move between players — every point someone wins comes out of someone else's pile. At the end of the session, whoever has the most points wins. There is no bonus added at the very end; your final pile **is** your score.

---

## Winning a hand — the basic payouts

When you win a hand, how you won decides who pays you:

**You drew the winning tile yourself (self-draw):**
Every one of the other three players pays you **2 points**. So a plain self-draw win earns you 6 points total.

**You won off someone's discarded tile:**
The person who threw the tile pays you **2 points**. The other two players each pay you **1 point**. A plain win this way earns you 4 points total.

**You stole the tile someone was adding to their kong ("robbing the kong"):**
The person making the kong pays you **everything** — all three shares by themselves (6 points on a plain hand). The other two players pay nothing.

**Heavenly win** (you're the dealer and your dealt hand is already a winner, before anyone has even played) or **Earthly win** (you win on the very first tile discarded in the hand): forget all the math — every player simply pays you a flat **20 points**. That's 60 points, and no bonuses below apply on top.

---

## Bonuses that multiply your win

These bonuses **multiply** the basic payouts above, and they stack with each other by multiplying again:

| Bonus                            | Effect                                                                                                         |
| -------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Seven Pairs hand                 | everything ×2                                                                                                  |
| All Triplets hand                | everything ×2                                                                                                  |
| Thirteen Misfits hand            | everything ×2                                                                                                  |
| Seven Star Thirteen Misfits hand | everything ×4                                                                                                  |
| German                           | everything ×2, **plus** every loser pays 5 extra points                                                        |
| True German                      | everything ×4, **plus** every loser pays 5 extra points (replaces the regular German bonus — they don't stack) |
| Spirit Fishing                   | everything ×2                                                                                                  |
| You are the dealer when you win  | everything ×2                                                                                                  |

One more rule about the dealer: if the **dealer throws** the tile that someone else wins on, the dealer's own payment is **doubled again**. Being the dealer is high risk, high reward.

### Worked examples

- Plain self-draw: 2 from each player → **6 points**.
- Dealer self-draws a Seven Pairs hand: 2 × 2 (pairs) × 2 (dealer) = 8 from each player → **24 points**.
- You win a German hand off a discard: the discarder pays 2 × 2 + 5 = **9**, the other two pay 1 × 2 + 5 = **7 each** → 23 points total.

---

## Kongs pay instantly

The moment you declare a kong, you collect — before the hand is even over, and you keep it even if you don't win:

- **Open kong** (using someone's discard, or adding the fourth tile to your own pung): **1 point from each player** → 3 points.
- **Concealed kong** (all four tiles from your own hand): **2 points from each player** → 6 points.

---

## Spirit tiles settle at the end of every hand

When the hand ends — **win, no-winner draw, doesn't matter** — everyone shows their spirit (jing) tiles and settles up. Count what you're holding, in your hand and in your melds:

- Each **primary spirit** tile: worth **2**
- Each **secondary spirit** tile: worth **1**
- A full **kong of spirit tiles**: worth an extra **10**

That total is your spirit score, and **each of the other three players pays you that many points** (and you pay each of them theirs).

Two twists:

- **Explosive Spirit:** if your spirit score reaches **5 or more**, it blows up — multiply it by (itself minus 3). A score of 5 becomes 10. A score of 6 becomes 18. A score of 7 becomes 28. Hoarding spirits pays off steeply.
- **Indomitable Spirit:** if you're the **only player at the table holding any spirits at all**, your score is **doubled** on top of everything.

---

## The opening flip (optional house rule)

If the "top & bottom flip" rule is switched on: right after the deal, one tile is flipped from the wall. Before a single tile is played:

- Anyone holding a copy of the **flipped tile** in their dealt hand collects **2 points per copy from each other player**.
- Anyone holding the **next tile up in sequence** collects **1 point per copy from each other player**.

Pure luck money — it's settled immediately and the hand then plays normally.

---

## Giving up

If you concede the hand, no points change hands at all. The hand simply ends (and the usual spirit settlement still happens).

---

## How the session ends

- The dealer **keeps the deal** when the dealer wins or the hand is a draw; otherwise the deal passes along.
- In a **rounds game**, you play until the East round (or East and South rounds) is complete. Most points wins.
- In a **bust game**, the session ends when a full rotation of deals finishes with someone's pile below zero. You can dip negative mid-round and claw back — elimination is only checked once the round is complete.
